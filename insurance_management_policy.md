# INSURANCE MANAGEMENT POLICY

## 1. INTRODUCTION

1.1. The purpose of this Insurance Management Policy is to establish a structured framework for managing insurance operations within the ZyroSafe platform.

1.2. ZyroSafe is a parametric insurance system designed to provide financial protection to food delivery partners against income loss caused by environmental or social disruptions.

1.3. The system operates using predefined disruption parameters and real-time external data sources such as weather APIs and event alerts to determine payout eligibility.

1.4. This policy ensures that insurance operations are conducted in a transparent, accountable and efficient manner while protecting both users and the platform.

---

## 2. RELATIONSHIP WITH OTHER POLICIES

2.1. This policy should be read together with other operational policies governing the ZyroSafe system, including the following:

(a) Data Privacy Policy – regulating the collection and processing of user data.

(b) Fraud Prevention Policy – defining mechanisms used to detect and prevent fraudulent claims.

(c) System Security Policy – governing system access control and data protection.

(d) Financial Management Policy – governing the management of premium pools and payouts.

2.2. This policy does not override other system governance policies. Where conflicts arise, the system administration team will review and update the relevant policies accordingly.

---

## 3. APPROVAL AND EFFECTIVE DATE

3.1. The system administration team is responsible for reviewing and approving the implementation of this policy.

3.2. The policy becomes effective upon deployment of the ZyroSafe insurance system and remains applicable throughout the system’s operational lifecycle.

---

## 4. POLICY IMPLEMENTATION

4.1. The implementation of this policy shall be carried out through automated system components responsible for disruption detection, payout calculation, and fraud validation.

4.2. System administrators shall ensure that all insurance operations comply with the rules and procedures defined in this policy.

---

## 5. POLICY AMENDMENT

5.1. This policy may be updated periodically to reflect changes in system functionality, operational requirements, or regulatory considerations.

5.2. Any amendments to this policy must be approved by the system administrators before implementation.

---

## 6. INSURANCE COVERAGE

6.1. The ZyroSafe platform provides insurance coverage to delivery partners who experience income disruption caused by verified external events.

6.2. Covered disruption conditions include:

(a) Heavy rainfall affecting delivery operations.

(b) Extreme heat conditions that make outdoor work unsafe.

(c) Cyclones or severe storms.

(d) Social disruptions such as curfews or strikes.

6.3. Disruption events must be validated using trusted external data sources before payouts are triggered.

---

## 7. ADDITIONS DURING THE YEAR

7.1. When new users enroll in the ZyroSafe insurance platform, their income data and working hours shall be recorded to determine premium calculations.

7.2. Any updates to a user’s insurance plan or working profile shall be recorded immediately within the system.

---

## 8. RECOVERY OF LOSS

8.1. In cases where incorrect payouts occur due to system errors or fraudulent activities, the system administration team shall initiate recovery procedures.

8.2. Fraudulent users may have their accounts suspended and may be required to return improperly obtained payouts.

---

## 9. PROCEDURES FOR CLAIM ADMINISTRATION

### GENERAL

9.1. ZyroSafe uses an automated claim administration process where claims are triggered based on verified disruption conditions.

9.2. The system automatically evaluates disruption parameters using real-time environmental and operational data.

9.3. All claims processed by the system shall be recorded in the transaction database for auditing purposes.

9.4. The system shall calculate payouts based on predefined formulas that consider income, working hours, and insurance plan factors.

---

## 10. CLAIM PROCEDURES

10.1. Claims are automatically triggered when disruption conditions are detected by the system.

10.2. The system shall perform the following steps when processing a claim:

(a) Capture user location data.

(b) Retrieve disruption data from external sources.

(c) Validate disruption conditions.

(d) Perform fraud detection checks.

(e) Calculate payout eligibility.

(f) Record the transaction in the system database.

10.3. Claims may result in one of the following outcomes:

(a) Approved – payout is issued.

(b) Flagged – claim requires further verification.

(c) Rejected – claim is considered invalid or fraudulent.

---

## 11. FRAUD PREVENTION

11.1. The system shall implement fraud detection mechanisms to prevent misuse of the insurance platform.

11.2. Fraud detection checks may include:

(a) GPS and IP location validation.

(b) Detection of location spoofing or VPN usage.

(c) Behavioral pattern analysis.

(d) Detection of repeated suspicious claims.

11.3. Each claim shall be assigned a fraud risk score used to determine the claim outcome.

---

## 12. RECORD KEEPING

12.1. All insurance transactions shall be stored in the system database for transparency and auditing purposes.

12.2. Recorded information may include:

(a) Transaction ID.

(b) Timestamp.

(c) Payout amount.

(d) Disruption type.

(e) Risk score.

(f) Claim status.

12.3. These records shall be used for monitoring system performance, resolving disputes, and improving fraud detection systems.

---

## 13. RESPONSIBILITIES

13.1. System administrators are responsible for maintaining the insurance platform and ensuring that all processes operate according to this policy.

13.2. Users of the system are responsible for providing accurate personal and income information when registering for insurance plans.

13.3. Users must enable location services to allow the system to validate disruption conditions.

13.4. Any attempt to manipulate the system or submit fraudulent information may result in account suspension or permanent removal from the platform.
